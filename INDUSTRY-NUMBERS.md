## Timeline Numbers

### Time from Request to COD
| Period | Median Duration |
|--------|-----------------|
| Projects built 2000-2007 | **<2 years** |
| Projects built 2015 | **3 years** |
| Projects built 2018-2024 | **4+ years** |
| Projects built 2023 | **5 years** |
| Current PJM average | **8+ years** |

### Study Phase Durations (Typical)
| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| Feasibility Study | 3-6 months |
| System Impact Study | 6-18 months |
| Facilities Study | 6-12 months |
| Construction/Interconnection | 12-24 months |
| **Total typical timeline** | **4-6 years** |

**Soundbite:** "In 2008, the average project took under two years from request to operation. Today in PJM, it's over eight years."

- **25%** = Direct interconnection costs (your equipment)

## FERC Order 2023 Key Points

### What Changed
| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| First-come, first-served (serial) | **First-ready, first-served (cluster)** |
| Low financial commitment upfront | **Higher deposits, milestone payments** |
| Easy to speculate in queue | **Commercial readiness requirements** |
| Unlimited study rounds | **Streamlined study process** |

### Timeline
| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 28, 2023 | Order 2023 issued |
| November 6, 2023 | Rule took effect |
| April 3, 2024 | Compliance filings deadline |
| May 16, 2024 | Order 2023-A compliance deadline |
| 2024-2025 | RTOs implementing new processes |
| 2026 | Most transition backlogs cleared |

### PJM Transition
- **~140 GW** of "transition projects" being processed under new clustered study
- Expected to clear backlog by **end of 2026**
- Approved **~46 GW** ready to build, not yet constructed
- Processing additional **63 GW** through 2026

**Soundbite:** "FERC Order 2023 is the biggest reform to interconnection in decades. It moves from 'first come' to 'first ready'—projects have to prove they're serious with money upfront."

## Useful Benchmarks

### Project Viability Quick Tests
| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| 5+ projects ahead at same POI | **High risk** - capacity likely constrained |
| Network upgrades >$200/kW | **Cost overrun risk** - above average |
| Developer with <50% completion rate | **Execution risk** - weak track record |
| Timeline <3 years from current phase | **Aggressive** - likely unrealistic |
| No PPA/offtake secured | **Financing risk** for generation projects |

### Developer Track Record Benchmarks
| Metric | Good | Caution | Red Flag |
|--------|------|---------|----------|
| Completion rate | >50% | 30-50% | <30% |
| Projects in queue | 3-10 | 10-20 | >20 (spread thin) |
| Avg time to COD | <4 years | 4-6 years | >6 years |

## Conversation Starters / Talking Points

### On Queue Backlogs
> "The US has 2,300 GW sitting in interconnection queues—that's nearly double the entire installed generation fleet. And only about 13% of those projects will ever reach operation."

### On Timelines
> "A decade ago, interconnection took about two years. Today in PJM, you're looking at eight years or more. And that's if everything goes well."

### On Costs
> "Interconnection costs have quadrupled since the 2000s. Solar projects in PJM are averaging $250/kW just for interconnection—and 75% of that is network upgrades."

### On Data Centers
> "Data center power demand is set to triple by 2030. We're talking about single campuses that need a gigawatt of power—that's a small city's worth of electricity for one building."

### On FERC Reform
> "Order 2023 is forcing projects to put real money down upfront. The speculative projects that have been clogging the queue for years? They're going to get flushed out."

### On Your Value
> "Developers tell you 2027 COD and $30M for interconnection. Our job is to tell you whether those numbers are real—and what the actual timeline and cost exposure look like."
