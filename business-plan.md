## The Problem

### What's Happening in the Market

The US electrical grid is facing unprecedented demand from data centers driven by AI:
- Single data center campus now requires 100MW-1GW+ of power
- PJM (largest RTO) has 250GW+ in interconnection queue, 90GW+ is data center load
- Wait times for new interconnection: 5-10 years
- Billions of dollars flowing into power infrastructure deals

### The Information Gap

**Buyers (PE funds, developers, lenders) face a problem:**

| What They Know | What They Don't Know |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Project is in queue | Will it actually get built? |
| Target COD is 2027 | Is that realistic? |
| Developer says costs are $30M | What are costs really? |
| Project looks good on paper | What are the hidden risks? |

**Current solutions are inadequate:**
- Hire Brattle Group / ICF for $50-150K engagement (overkill for many deals)
- Trust the developer's claims (risky)
- DIY analysis (they don't have expertise)
- Skip diligence (bad outcomes)

### Why Now

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| AI boom | Unprecedented data center demand |
| Capital influx | PE/infra funds deploying billions |
| Queue congestion | Uncertainty at all-time high |
| Utility overwhelm | Information gaps everywhere |
| Regulatory complexity | FERC reforms, state policies shifting |

## Pricing Strategy

### Pricing Philosophy

PE funds don't optimize for saving $2,500—they optimize for not looking stupid to their IC. A $5,000 report that gives them cover is worth more than a $2,500 report that feels "cheap." Price signals credibility.

### Standard Pricing

| Service | Price | When to Use |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Quick Screen | $1,500-2,000 | Same-day pass/fail, low barrier to try you |
| Full Report | $4,000-5,000 | Standard engagements, core product |
| Comparative | $7,500-10,000 | Multiple sites/projects |
| Portfolio | $15,000-20,000 | Fund-level analysis |
| Retainer | $6,000-8,000/mo | Repeat clients, includes 2 reports + questions |

### Early Client Pricing (First 3-5 Clients)

| Service | Standard | Early Adopter | Framing |
|---------|----------|---------------|---------|
| Full Report | $5,000 | $3,500 | "In exchange for being a reference" |
| Comparative | $10,000 | $7,000 | "Beta pricing while we build case studies" |

**Key:** Always quote the standard rate first, then offer the discount. This establishes value and creates reciprocity.

### Value Justification

```
Deal size: $80M data center acquisition
Your fee: $5,000
Fee as % of deal: 0.006%

Alternative: Brattle Group engagement
Their fee: $75,000+
Your fee: $5,000
Savings: 93%

Value delivered: Identified $15M cost overrun risk
Client action: Renegotiated price, saved $10M
ROI on your fee: 2,000x
```

### Pricing Psychology

| Price Point | Signal | PE Reaction |
|-------------|--------|-------------|
| $500-1,500 | Side project, amateur | "Is this even real?" |
| $2,000-3,000 | Freelancer, light work | "Might be useful for a quick check" |
| $4,000-5,000 | Professional service | "This is a real offering" |
| $7,500-15,000 | Serious advisory | "Comparable to other vendors we use" |

**Never price below $1,500 for anything.** Below that threshold, you signal "not a real business." You're not competing on price—you're competing on speed, expertise, and credibility.
