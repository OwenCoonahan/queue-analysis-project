# Competitive Analysis: Interconnection Queue Analysis Services

*Last Updated: January 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

The interconnection consulting market is served by a mix of large consultancies, engineering firms, and boutique specialists. Key insight: **no one is specifically positioned as a fast, affordable queue feasibility service for deal diligence.** This is the gap.

| Segment | Players | Typical Engagement | Your Position |
|---------|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Big Consultancies** | Brattle, ICF | $50-150K+, 4-8 weeks | Too expensive, too slow |
| **Engineering Firms** | Sargent & Lundy, GE Vernova | $25-75K, technical focus | Too technical, not deal-focused |
| **Data Platforms** | Wood Mackenzie, GridStatus | $5-50K/year subscription | Data only, no analysis |
| **Boutique Consultants** | Aneden, GridSME, EPE | $10-30K per project | Closer competition but still slow |
| **You** | Queue feasibility | $4-5K, 48-72 hours | Fast, focused, right-sized |

## Key Competitive Insights

### 1. The Gap You Fill
No one offers fast, affordable queue feasibility analysis specifically for deal diligence. Brattle/ICF are overkill. Data platforms don't give recommendations. Boutiques are still slow and expensive.

### 2. Complementary, Not Competitive
Position as complementary to big consultancies:
- "For full technical due diligence, hire Sargent & Lundy"
- "For a quick queue feasibility check before you spend $50K, start with us"

### 3. Speed Is Your Weapon
Deal timelines are tight. Being able to deliver in 48-72 hours is a massive advantage when a fund needs to decide on a bid by Friday.

### 4. Price Anchoring
At $4-5K, you're:
- 90-95% cheaper than Brattle
- 50-80% cheaper than boutiques
- Priced to signal professional, credible work

### 5. Credibility Building
Your weakness is brand recognition. Build through:
- Case studies and testimonials
- "State of the Queue" thought leadership
- Trade publication citations
- LinkedIn content
